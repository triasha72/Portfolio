Why it matters

Aviation decarbonization is not only a vehicle or fuel problem. Feedstock logistics, conversion pathways, distribution, regulatory targets, and environmental burden all shape whether a low-carbon pathway is actually equitable and scalable.

What I contributed

  • Framed the SAF pathway as a full system instead of an isolated conversion process.
  • Connected forest residue sourcing, transport, conversion, and distribution to carbon-intensity outcomes.
  • Built a planning-facing interpretation: who bears logistics burdens, where benefits concentrate, and what metrics matter beyond average emissions.

Planning bridge

This is the strongest bridge to SCaRP because it translates engineering sustainability modeling into questions about spatial distribution, access, policy, and environmental justice — the same kind of systems framing used in sustainable mobility research.
